Introduction

The Fibonacci sequence \(F_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2}\) with \(F_0 = 0, F_1 = 1\) defines a spiral that converges to the golden ratio \(\phi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \approx 1.618\).

Geometric Interpretation

Each quarter-circle arc in the spiral has radius \(F_n\), producing the recurrence: \[r_n = r_{n-1} + r_{n-2}\]

The cumulative arc length approaches \(\phi \cdot r_n\) as \(n \to \infty\).

Visualization Method

The rendered animation plots 3D coordinates \((x_n, y_n, z_n)\) derived from the polar form \(r = e^{b\theta}\) where \(b = \frac{\ln \phi}{\pi/2}\).
